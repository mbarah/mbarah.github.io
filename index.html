About Me

I'm Masoud Barah, a scientist specializing in Operations Research and Statistical Modeling. My expertise lies in Integer Programming and Statistical Modeling, allowing me to develop robust solutions for complex problems. My research, significantly impacting the scientific community, emphasizes practical application of integer programming for scheduling and resource allocation and statistical modeling for uncertainty management. I thrive on challenging problems and the application of mathematical models to resolve them.

Education

  • Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering (minor in Statistics), GPA: 4.0, University of Tennessee, Knoxville, TN. Dissertation on the stochastic equitable resource sharing/allocation systems
  • M.Sc. in Systems Engineering, GPA: 4.0, Amirkabir University of Technology (Tehran Polytechnic), Tehran, Iran. Dissertation on stochastic time-discretized network optimization
  • B.Sc. in Industrial Engineering, GPA: 3.19, Khajeh Nasir University of Technology, Tehran, Iran
